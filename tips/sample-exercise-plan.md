# Tip: Sample exercise plan (reference only)

**For non-clinical participants:** Use this simplified, evidence-based progression as **reference data** for demos—sample schedules, AI coach tone, or gamification levels. In real care, **only the care team prescribes** exercise; your solution should **support** a plan like this, not replace clinical judgment.

---

## The talk test

The simplest way many programs gauge intensity: if the patient can **carry on a conversation** while exercising but **couldn’t sing**, they’re usually in an appropriate zone. If they **can’t talk comfortably**, they’re pushing too hard.

## Weeks 1–2: Getting started

- Walk **10–15 minutes** at a comfortable pace, **3–4 days per week**  
- **5-minute** slow warm-up and **5-minute** cool-down  
- Intensity: **light**; patient can talk easily  
- If tolerated, add a **second short walk** later in the day (e.g. two 10-minute walks)  

## Weeks 3–4: Building duration

- Walking **20–25 minutes**, **4–5 days per week**  
- Optional: **stationary cycling** 10–15 minutes at low resistance  
- **Light resistance** (bands or bodyweight): seated leg extensions, wall push-ups, bicep curls (2–5 lb)—**one set** of 10–15 reps per exercise, **2 days per week**  

## Weeks 5–8: Progressing intensity

- Aerobic: **30–40 minutes**, **5 days per week**  
- Pace: **moderate**—can talk but not sing  
- Variety: walking, cycling, or light swimming  
- Resistance: **2 sets** per exercise, **2–3 days per week**  
- Structure: warm-up **5–10 min**, conditioning, cool-down **5–10 min**, stretching  

## Weeks 9–12: Reaching maintenance

- Aim for **30–60 minutes** of moderate aerobic activity **5 days per week** (about **150 minutes per week** total)  
- Resistance training **2–3 days per week** with gradually increasing load  
- Goal: patient feels **confident exercising independently** by the end of this phase  

## What to track along the way

Useful data for progress and motivation (your app might capture these—not as diagnosis):

- **Duration and frequency** of exercise  
- **Perceived effort** (could they talk comfortably?)  
- **Symptoms** during or after activity  
- **Mood** before and after sessions  
- **Completion** of the day’s planned activity  

Remember: **personalization comes from the care team**; your demo can illustrate structure and support, not clinical prescribing.
