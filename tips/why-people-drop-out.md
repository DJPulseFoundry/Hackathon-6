# Tip: Why people drop out

**For non-clinical participants:** Low participation is usually about barriers and context—not “laziness.” Design against these friction points.

---

## Common barriers

Your prototype can target:

- Distance to a rehab facility and lack of transportation  
- Work or caregiving conflicts  
- Out-of-pocket costs  
- Difficulty understanding medical instructions  
- Language barriers  
- Depression or anxiety after a cardiac event  
- A doctor who didn’t strongly recommend rehab  
- The belief that rehab isn’t necessary once symptoms go away  

## Takeaway

These are **systemic and emotional** friction points—not just lack of motivation. Solutions that only nudge harder without removing logistics, cost, clarity, or fear often miss why people never start or stop coming.
