# Tip: How cardiac rehab works

**For non-clinical participants:** Cardiac rehab is a structured clinical program. Knowing its phases helps you align your solution with how care is actually delivered.

---

## What it is

Cardiac rehab is a medically supervised recovery program prescribed after a heart attack, heart surgery, stent placement, or heart failure diagnosis. The standard course is **36 one-hour sessions** over about **12 weeks**, led by a team of cardiologists, nurses, exercise specialists, dietitians, and psychologists.

## Three phases

| Phase | Where | What happens |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Phase I** | In the hospital | Gentle mobilization, education, and discharge planning. |
| **Phase II** | Outpatient (core program) | Supervised sessions about **three times per week**: monitored exercise, nutrition coaching, stress management, and risk factor education. |
| **Phase III** | Lifelong maintenance | Patients continue on their own. **This is where most people drop off.** |
